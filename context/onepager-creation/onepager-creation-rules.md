# One Pager Creation Rules

## Core Structural Pattern

### Required Sections (in order)

#### 1. Opening Hook
**Purpose**: Establish the problem context and position Giant Swarm as the strategic solution

**Structure**:
- **Problem Statement**: "Platform teams face [specific challenge/choice]..."
- **Inadequate Alternatives**: "You can [expensive/time-consuming option] or [inadequate option]..."  
- **Giant Swarm Positioning**: "Giant Swarm offers a third, more effective path."

**Requirements**:
- Start with audience pain point, not product features
- Present the classic "false choice" between two poor alternatives
- Position Giant Swarm as the strategic third option

#### 2. Value Proposition Statement
**Purpose**: Clearly articulate the core benefit and differentiation

**Pattern**:
```
Leverage our deep platform engineering expertise to deploy a [adjective], 
[adjective], and [adjective] [solution type] that is [benefit 1], [benefit 2], 
and [benefit 3]. It's the same battle-tested [solution] we use to [proof point], 
designed to [specific customer benefit].
```

**Key Elements**:
- "Leverage our deep platform engineering expertise"
- Three descriptive adjectives (modular, production-ready, proven, etc.)
- Three key benefits
- "Battle-tested" proof point
- Specific customer outcome

#### 3. Why Giant Swarm Section
**Purpose**: Present 3-4 compelling value propositions with consistent themes

**Required Value Props (choose 3-4)**:

**Acceleration Value Prop** (always include):
- **Pattern**: "Accelerate Your [Specific Outcome]: [Explanation of time/resource benefits]"
- **Focus**: Years of effort → immediate value
- **Example**: "Accelerate Your Security Posture: Building a comprehensive security framework can take years..."

**Expertise Value Prop** (always include):
- **Pattern**: "Leverage True Platform Engineering Expertise: [Differentiation explanation]"
- **Focus**: Not just tools, but embedded expertise
- **Key phrases**: "platform engineering experts, for platform engineering experts", "we live and breathe this"

**Control/Sovereignty Value Prop** (include when relevant):
- **Pattern**: "[Maintain/Ensure] [Control/Security/Sovereignty]: [Explanation of data/environment control]"
- **Focus**: Your environment, your data, compliance built-in
- **Example**: "Maintain Full Data Sovereignty and Control: Unlike SaaS platforms..."

**Focused Solution Value Prop** (include when relevant):
- **Pattern**: "Get the Essentials Without the [Overhead/Complexity]: [Explanation of focused approach]"
- **Focus**: Curated, essential capabilities vs. bloated enterprise suites
- **Example**: "Get the Essentials Without the Overhead: We provide the core pillars..."

#### 4. Technical Solution Overview
**Purpose**: Provide credible technical details while maintaining accessibility

**Structure**:
- Brief intro paragraph positioning the offering
- 3-4 core technical components
- Each component: **Tool/Technology**: Description of managed capability

**Requirements**:
- Use specific tool names for credibility (Grafana, Kubernetes, etc.)
- Emphasize "we deploy and manage" approach
- Focus on integrated solution, not individual tools
- Keep descriptions accessible but technically accurate

#### 5. How It Works Process
**Purpose**: Demonstrate partnership approach and implementation process

**Required 3-Step Structure**:

**Step 1: Initial Assessment**
- "Our engagement [starts/begins] with a collaborative assessment..."
- Emphasize partnership and customization
- Focus on understanding specific customer needs

**Step 2: Tailored Deployment and Configuration**  
- "We deploy and configure the [solution] into your environment..."
- Emphasize integration with existing infrastructure
- Highlight customization and seamless integration

**Step 3: Ongoing Management and Support**
- "Giant Swarm provides continuous, hands-on management..."
- Include "24/7 expert support" and "extension of your team"
- Emphasize ongoing value and evolution

#### 6. Call-to-Action
**Purpose**: Drive next step engagement with outcome-focused messaging

**Structure**:
- **Outcome Question**: "Ready to [achieve specific outcome] without [specific pain point]?"
- **Simple CTA**: "[Contact Us to Learn More]"

**Requirements**:
- Frame as achieving positive outcome, not just solving problem
- Keep CTA simple and accessible
- Maintain focus on customer benefit

## Content Guidelines

### Tone and Voice Requirements

**Professional but Accessible**:
- Avoid excessive jargon while maintaining technical credibility
- Use direct address ("you", "your") to engage reader
- Balance business benefits with technical details

**Problem-First Narrative**:
- Always start with customer pain points
- Position Giant Swarm as strategic alternative, not just vendor
- Emphasize practical benefits over feature lists

**Expertise Positioning**:
- Consistently reference platform engineering expertise
- Use phrases like "platform engineering experts, for platform engineering experts"
- Emphasize real-world experience and proven solutions

### Key Messaging Themes

#### 1. Time to Value
- Emphasize acceleration: "years of effort" → "now"
- Focus on avoiding build time and immediate benefits
- Highlight resource reallocation to higher-value work

#### 2. Expertise Transfer
- Position as embedded expertise, not just tools
- Use phrases: "we live and breathe this stack", "proven at scale"
- Emphasize learning from Giant Swarm's operational experience

#### 3. Control and Sovereignty
- Your environment, your data, your control
- Compliance and security built-in
- Avoid vendor lock-in concerns

#### 4. Focused Solutions
- "Essentials without overhead/complexity"
- Curated vs. comprehensive approach
- Platform engineering focus, not everything-for-everyone

### Language Requirements

**Required Phrases and Patterns**:
- "Platform engineering expertise" (use consistently)
- "Battle-tested" or "proven at scale" (for credibility)
- "Deploy and manage" (for service positioning)
- "Extension of your team" (for partnership positioning)

**Avoid**:
- Generic vendor speak ("cutting-edge", "revolutionary")
- Over-technical jargon without context
- Feature lists without customer benefit connection
- Vague benefits without specific outcomes

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing any one-pager, verify:

### Structure and Content
- [ ] Opening hook follows problem → alternatives → Giant Swarm pattern
- [ ] Value proposition includes expertise leverage and battle-tested proof point
- [ ] Why Giant Swarm section has 3-4 value props with required themes
- [ ] Technical solution provides credible details with specific tools
- [ ] How It Works follows 3-step partnership structure
- [ ] CTA frames positive outcome and includes simple action

### Messaging and Tone
- [ ] Starts with customer pain points, not product features
- [ ] Consistently positions platform engineering expertise
- [ ] Balances technical credibility with accessibility
- [ ] Uses direct, engaging language throughout
- [ ] Avoids jargon without losing technical credibility

### Giant Swarm Brand Alignment
- [ ] Emphasizes platform engineering focus and expertise
- [ ] Positions as strategic partner, not just vendor
- [ ] Maintains professional, expert tone
- [ ] Includes proof points and credibility indicators
- [ ] Aligns with company positioning and values

### Customer Focus
- [ ] Addresses specific target audience pain points
- [ ] Clearly articulates customer benefits and outcomes
- [ ] Positions solution as addressing real operational challenges
- [ ] Demonstrates understanding of customer environment and needs

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Feature-First Approach**: Starting with what the product does rather than what problem it solves
2. **Generic Pain Points**: Using vague problems instead of specific platform team challenges
3. **Tool Lists**: Presenting disconnected tools rather than integrated solution
4. **Vendor Speak**: Using generic marketing language instead of specific, credible benefits
5. **Technical Jargon**: Over-complicating language without providing business context
6. **Weak CTAs**: Using vague actions instead of specific, outcome-focused next steps
7. **Missing Expertise Positioning**: Failing to emphasize platform engineering expertise differentiation

## What NOT to Include

- Deep technical implementation details better suited for documentation
- Extensive feature comparisons with competitors
- Pricing information (belongs in sales conversations)
- Generic marketing claims without specific proof points
- Complex diagrams or technical architectures
- Multiple CTAs that dilute the primary action
- Internal Giant Swarm terminology without explanation
